The Literal Icon, Chinua Achebe

Here is the biography of Chinua Achebe

  • Chinua Achebe was a Nigerian novelist, poet, and essayist, widely considered to be one of Africa's most influential writers.
  • He was born on November 16, 1930, in Ogidi, Nigeria, and passed away on March 21, 2013, in Boston, Massachusetts, United States.
  • Achebe's best-known work is his debut novel, "Things Fall Apart," published in 1958, which has become a classic of modern African literature and has been translated into over 50 languages.
  • Throughout his career, Achebe wrote several other novels, including "No Longer at Ease," "Arrow of God," and "Anthills of the Savannah," among others.
  • He was a vocal critic of colonialism and its effects on African societies, and his writing often explored themes of cultural conflict and identity.
  • Achebe was also an influential literary critic and essayist, and his work helped to shape the field of African literature and its place within the wider canon of world literature.
  • In recognition of his contributions to literature and cultural discourse, Achebe received numerous awards and honors throughout his life, including the Man Booker International Prize, the Commonwealth Poetry Prize, and the Nigerian National Merit Award, among others.
  • Achebe's legacy continues to be celebrated and studied by readers and scholars around the world, and his work remains an important touchstone for contemporary African literature and culture.
